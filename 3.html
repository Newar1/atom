Chapter 1: Some Basic Concepts of Chemistry

Importance of Chemistry

  • Agriculture and Food:
    • Fertilizers: Urea, calcium phosphate, ammonium phosphate
    • Insecticides, fungicides, pesticides
    • Preservatives for jams, butter, squashes
  • Health and Sanitation:
    • Drugs: Penicillin, sulpha drugs, AZT (AIDS), Cisplatin (cancer)
    • Disinfectants: Phenol for toilets and drains
    • Chlorine for water sterilization (0.2 to 0.4 ppm)
  • Environment: Substitutes like CNG help reduce pollution
  • Industry: Production of dyes, soaps, polymers, drugs, etc.

Matter

Anything that has mass and occupies space.

Classification of Matter

Physical Classification

  • Solids: Definite shape and volume
  • Liquids: Definite volume, no definite shape
  • Gases: No definite shape or volume

Chemical Classification

  • Pure Substances
    • Elements: Made of same type of atoms or molecules
    • Compounds: Fixed ratio of elements; can be decomposed
  • Mixtures
    • Homogeneous: Uniform composition (e.g., sugar water)
    • Heterogeneous: Visible separation (e.g., iron + sulphur)

Differences Between Compounds and Mixtures

Compounds Mixtures
Elements combined chemically Mixed without chemical bonding
Fixed ratio by mass Variable composition
Always homogeneous Can be homogeneous or heterogeneous
Cannot be separated physically Can be separated physically
No identity of constituents remains Constituents retain properties
