Some Basic Concepts of Chemistry

Importance of Chemistry

  • In Agriculture and Food:
    • Fertilizers: urea, calcium phosphate, sodium nitrate
    • Insecticides, fungicides, pesticides
    • Food preservation (jam, butter, squashes)
  • In Health and Sanitation:
    • Sulpha drugs, penicillin, cisplatin, taxol, AZT
    • Disinfectants: phenol
    • Chlorine (0.2–0.4 ppm) for water sterilization
  • Saving the Environment:
    • Reducing pollution: CNG as petrol substitute
  • Application in Industry:
    • Fertilizers, dyes, detergents, alloys, polymers

Matter

Anything that has mass and occupies space.

Classification of Matter

  • Physical Classification:
    • Solids: Definite shape and volume
    • Liquids: Definite volume, no definite shape
    • Gases: No definite shape or volume
  • Chemical Classification:
    • Pure Substances
      • Elements: metals, non-metals, semi-metals
      • Compounds: inorganic, organic
    • Mixtures
      • Homogeneous: uniform composition
      • Heterogeneous: visible boundaries

Differences between Compounds and Mixtures

  1. Compounds: chemically combined; Mixtures: physically combined
  2. Fixed ratio in compounds; variable in mixtures
  3. Compounds: homogeneous; Mixtures: homogeneous or heterogeneous
  4. Compounds: not separated by physical methods; mixtures can
  5. Compounds lose identities; mixtures retain identities

• Properties of Matter and Units of Measurement

  • Physical Properties: Can be measured without changing the identity of the substance. Examples: colour, odour, melting point, boiling point.
  • Chemical Properties: Require a chemical change. Examples: acidity, basicity, combustibility.
  • Fundamental Units: Basic measurable quantities like mass, length, time.
  • SI System: Most widely used system. It includes 7 base quantities:
    • Length (metre - m)
    • Mass (kilogram - kg)
    • Time (second - s)
    • Temperature (kelvin - K)
    • Amount of substance (mole - mol)
    • Electric current (ampere - A)
    • Luminous intensity (candela - cd)

✔️ Tip: Learn the 7 SI units and physical vs chemical properties clearly.
